Volenteering at MEET

MEET is founded on the belief that the lives of Palestinians and Israelis are inherently intertwined - we share common resources like water and land. ... However, the current isolation, dehumanization and unequal access to skills between the sides has led Palestinians and Israelis to lose hope and become apathetic to each other's needs and fears. With growing disillusionment on both sides, cultivating cross-border relationships is now harder and more pressing than ever. Being a volenteer at MEET taught me skills to further my career in teaching. It gave me the skills I needed in order to be a leader in my own community. To start my own coding programs in both the Ahliya University and Dheisha Refugee Camp.

Teaching at Ahliya University

The Coding Academy was a collaboration between Palestine Ahliya University and the Jerusalem School Bethlehem, with a goal of teaching 15-20 young adults in Palestine to build web applications. ... Students learned full-stack web development in JavaScript, including front-end frameworks like React, API calls, and how to setup back-end servers and databases. We tried to take students who had little to no experience in programming to provide work opportunity for them in the future. At the end of the course I was certified to teach coding at the Ahliya University.
